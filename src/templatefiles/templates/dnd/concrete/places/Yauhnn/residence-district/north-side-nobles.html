The residents in this area are clearly wealthy. Among many big houses you also see buildings that appear to be used as offices. The buildings often hold marble signs that indicate which business is situated inside. On a square there is a bronze statue of a donkey. It barks and has its tail upright and beneath its ass stands a bag with coins. The inscription reads: "the golden age is coming"

In front of one building that reads Faerun Trade Group a man is talking to a closed door. He says that he wants to cancel his investment and that he needs his money now. He seems quite desparate, but the door remains shut.

It is hard to distinguish one house from another and locations below can't be reached unless they know they want to go there.

  • The house of Dralen
  • The house of Wehna (clothing merchant).
  • The house of Harar (wood merchant).
  • House of Lapoho (head of the city guards)
  • Faerun Trade Group headquarters.
  • The most notable accountants agency.

From here adventurers can go to:

  • Down the ramps to The goods
  • Across the walking bridges over the market to south side nobles
  • Towards the richer houses in Captain huts
  • North entrance of the big market
  • The fort

Stealing

It is feasible to make a profit out of robbing some of the nobles here. In one night a rogue can make 1d20 * 300 in an evening in this area.

Stealing is never without risks and every night there is probably an incident where the rogue almost gets caught. After stealing 10.000 euro in the city there starts to a lot more awareness. Per 10.000 gp there is a +10% change that a night goes horribly wrong and the rogue gets caught successfully.

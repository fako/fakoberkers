Walking around the area you feel that something is not right. When you look a little closer it is inevitable that at some point an illusion breaks and reveals the true neighborhood. The houses here are very poorly build. It seems like things needed to be as cheap as possible.

On a small square in the middle of the neighborhood on top of a small wooden box somebody is shouting out towards the people. Not a lot of people seem to take interest. When you start listening it seems to be a cleric of Waukeen (called Youra) who states that buying something cheaply will end up in regret. If you buy something you should make sure it is the best deal that you can get in order for the society as a whole to thrive. Then he raves on that you should not make a living out of the promise of goods. Goods are there or they are not, here and now. Do not think about what might be tomorrow, do not dream about its promises, for tomorrow changes as the weather. Unexpectedly violent.

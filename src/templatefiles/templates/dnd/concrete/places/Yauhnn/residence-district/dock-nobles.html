Walking around the area you feel that something is not right. When you look a little closer it is inevitable that at some point an illusion breaks and reveals the true neighborhood. The houses here are poorly build. Not much different from the harbor slums on the other side of the bay. The big difference is that you see the gloom of illusions hang around the houses. Illusions designed to make the houses look a lot more valuable than they are.

The illusions break the most near the cliff face. Some illusion walls mean to cover up the entrances to mines there. However the workers keep on moving the dug up materials into warehouses. The air is thick with mining dust.

It used to be a poor part of the city, but thanks to FTG it is blooming business.
